Our story

A journal grown
from curiosity
and quiet mornings.

Foliage began as a handwritten notebook kept by a forest ecologist who had too many thoughts and not enough people willing to hear them at breakfast.

It has since become a small, independent journal about the natural world — the growing, the foraging, the fermenting, and the slow art of paying close attention to what the earth is quietly doing around us.

What we believe

The Foliage Manifesto

We are not a news site. We are not a brand. We are not trying to sell you a lifestyle. We are a small group of people who find the natural world endlessly interesting and believe that slow, careful writing is one of the most radical things left to do.

Slowness is not a flaw

We publish when something is ready. We do not chase the news cycle. A well-observed piece about a single oak tree is worth more to us than a trend roundup written in an afternoon.

Place matters

Every story we publish is grounded in a specific landscape. We resist the universal claim. The land you stand on is not the same as the land we stand on, and that difference is the whole point.

Science and wonder are not opposites

We are rigorous about facts and unembarrassed about awe. Knowing the name of a thing does not diminish it. It usually deepens it considerably.

No advertisements. Ever.

Foliage is funded entirely by readers. We are grateful for that trust and intend never to abuse it. What you read here has not been shaped by a sponsor's interest.

The small is worth noticing

A beetle. A particular smell before rain. The way a field looks in November. We believe these things deserve the same quality of attention as anything else.

Our journey

Six years of growing slowly

Every year has brought a new voice, a new discipline, a new corner of the natural world we had not properly considered before.

Spring 2019

The first issue

Elara publishes the first Foliage piece — a 4,000-word essay on the social lives of beech trees — from her kitchen table in Edinburgh. 340 people read it.

Autumn 2019

The first collaborators

Theo and Mira join. The journal begins covering foraging and garden life alongside ecology. The first newsletter goes out to 800 subscribers.

Summer 2021

Reader-supported

We quietly remove all advertising and launch a reader subscription. 1,200 people pay within the first month. We cry about it, not quietly.

Spring 2023

Press recognition

The Guardian names Foliage one of the "ten independent publications quietly doing important things." We frame it and pretend not to care.

Today · 2025

Still here, still slow

24,000 newsletter readers. 12 writers. 340 stories. One small team that still finds the natural world completely astonishing.

"We are not trying to go viral. We are trying to go deep."

Elara Woods, Founder
